The Trap Inception

What's a trap? Or better — what makes a trap? Or even better — what comes first to your mind when you hear the word trap? There are many traps and everywhere. Here, as the title suggests, the focus is in a single one that all have in common.

Trap always comes with a bait. In mice’s case is the cheese. Mice are great smellers. So great that if they smell us around a trap, they will avoid that trap. Poor mice perceive a trap as a threat only when humans are present. Thinking that way makes them confident to get the cheese when we are absent. All of that just to get trapped by something they can’t smell. If however mice somehow survive, they try to avoid repeating the same actions by remembering what got them into trouble. They also warn other mice about it either way. Yet they get trapped no matter what…

Let's face reality. What makes ground for a trap is the ignorance of its existence. Unlike mice, we humans are able to develop cognitive skills. Hence, we are able to distinguish potential traps and avoid them. Half truth; we may avoid the traps we distinguish, but we don't avoid traps we don't distinguish. So it’s not enough to know a trap exists, but it’s an advantage if utilized properly. For mice the traps are simple, for us humans are a lot more complex. Ours are composed of hypotheses, biases, fallacies, paradoxes and many more. You can’t see, smell or feel any of these. You have to experience them mentally.

So what to do? No matter how hard you try to avoid them, you’ll eventually fall for them. Instead; observe, experiment and try to exploit them for your own benefit. There’s no way around, anticipate the entrapment and be prepared to escape it. Under those circumstances be sure to do so as early as possible. Why? In real life traps the catch occurs at a later time, which result in less vigilance and more dangerousness. The bigger you get, the deeper you fell and the harder you get out. As always, there is an exception to the rule: One-way traps. Avoid traps that threaten your own life at all costs. Full stop.

A human would never get caught in mouse’s trap and vice versa. Out there is a trap for everyone to get lost in the sauce. Hopefully just like mistakes, the smart learns from its own while the wise from other’s. If there is nobody to learn from, that lesson is yours. In the end bad things turn out to be good things uh? But that’s for another post…

Keep wondering but a trap can’t stop you from getting your cheese!
